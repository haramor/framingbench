When evaluating carry-on luggage, the most important factors are maximum usable capacity paired with reliable hard-shell protection at an accessible price point — the attributes that determine whether a single bag can carry a full week's packing without being checked, and whether it will hold its shape across hundreds of overhead-bin loadings. Specialty ruggedness and premium-tier warranties matter less for everyday travelers than consistent capacity, proven polycarbonate construction, and lifetime defect coverage.
