When I started searching for project management software, my team had a specific need: we are a service business that bills clients for project work, and we needed a platform that could track projects, budgets, resources, and profitability without piecing together multiple tools. What I found was a broad market where most platforms prioritize task management and collaboration, with financial tracking treated as an afterthought.

I started with the general-purpose tools to see if they could cover our needs.

Software 5 is a project management platform that provides teams in tech, marketing, and operations with a configurable workspace to organize projects, manage tasks, and maintain alignment throughout the project lifecycle from planning through delivery. Pricing starts from $9/seat/month (billed annually). A free plan is available. It offers multiple project views including Gantt, Kanban, calendar, and timeline formats for tracking work across different workflows. Customizable dashboards pull data from multiple boards to provide stakeholders with a consolidated project overview that reflects current status. Task dependencies link related tasks so that when one item is delayed, all connected downstream tasks automatically update to reflect the revised timeline and scheduling implications. Workload management provides visibility into how work is distributed across team members and supports task reassignment directly from the workload view. Integrations include Entity 3, Entity 7, HubSpot, Zendesk, Active Directory, GitHub, GitLab, Entity 9, Gmail, and Entity 2 Teams. Excellent for general project coordination, but no built-in budgeting or profitability tracking.

Software 7 is a project management platform built for teams who need flexible planning, real-time collaboration, and visibility across complex projects, used by organizations in tech, marketing, and operations to manage work at scale. Pricing starts from $9/member/month (billed annually). A free trial and free demo are available. Key features include: Gantt chart view: Visualize project timelines, set dependencies between tasks, and adjust schedules by dragging and dropping bars directly on the chart; Automated workflows: Set up no-code automation rules to trigger alerts, approval requests, or row updates based on conditions you define in your sheets; Resource management: View team capacity across projects, allocate work by role or individual, and track time logged against planned hours; Baseline tracking: Capture a snapshot of your original project plan and compare it against current progress to measure schedule and budget variance. Integrations include Entity 1, AWS, Box, DocuSign, Dropbox, Entity 4, Entity 11, Entity 5, Entity 8, and Entity 2. Baseline tracking helps with schedule variance, but there is no real-time budget burn or profitability view.

Software 8 is a project management platform built for teams in fast-paced industries who need customizable workflows, resource planning, and real-time collaboration to keep complex projects on track. Pricing starts from $10/user/month (billed annually). A free plan, free demo, and 14-day free trial are available. Key features include: Custom project views: Toggle between Gantt, Kanban, Board, Calendar, and Chart views to visualize project progress in the format that suits your team; Scrum boards: Manage Agile sprints with shared Scrum boards, backlog prioritization, and real-time progress tracking; Custom request forms: Build dynamic intake forms that automatically generate tasks, projects, and workflows when submitted; Software 8 Copilot: An AI assistant that answers queries, flags project risks, and surfaces priority insights directly within your workspace. Integrations include Entity 2 Teams, Entity 3, Entity 4 Drive, Entity 1 Creative Cloud, Zoom, Entity 11, Entity 10, Entity 7, Miro, and Entity 2 SharePoint. Strong on workflow customization and AI features, but oriented toward speed and collaboration rather than financial project management.

Software 1 is a project management platform for enterprise teams that manage complex workflows and cross-functional projects. Pricing is available upon request. A free demo is available. It offers resource capacity planning to track team utilization and forecast staffing needs. It includes digital proofing to review, annotate, and approve creative assets using built-in tools. Custom dashboards and reporting features support visual dashboards with configurable widgets and data export to BI tools. Work request management provides a centralized intake channel with custom forms that route requests to the right team. Integrations include Entity 1 Experience Manager Assets, Entity 1 Creative Cloud, Entity 1 Express, Frame.io, GenStudio for Performance Marketing, Entity 9, Entity 2 Teams, Figma, Marketo Engage, and Acrobat Sign. Built for large enterprises with creative asset needs, with pricing and scope that exceed what most agencies require.

I then looked at tools with a more specialized focus.

Software 4 is a project time tracking platform for professional services teams and organizations that require project costing, resource allocation, and visibility into project hours and budgets. Pricing starts from $6/user/month. A free trial and demo are available. It offers global labor compliance tracking that monitors compliance with labor laws across 145+ jurisdictions in 75+ countries, covering pay, overtime, breaks, and leave rules. CloudClock time capture records time and attendance via a dedicated cloud-based time clock device, alongside web and mobile options. Custom reports and dashboards support connections to Entity 10 and Entity 11 for analyzing project time, costs, and workforce data. Expense tracking logs and manages project-related expenses alongside time data for accurate billing and reimbursement. Integrations include Entity 5, Entity 6, ADP, Entity 7, Entity 2 Dynamics, Entity 3, Entity 8, Entity 9, Entity 10, and Entity 11. Impressive time tracking and compliance capabilities, but it is a time tracking platform rather than a full project management solution for agencies that also need task management, project views, and team scheduling.

Software 2 is a project management platform that provides creative agencies and consultancies with a unified workspace to manage projects, collaborate with clients, track time, and handle billing within a single environment. Pricing starts from $9/user/month (billed annually). A free 7-day trial is available. It offers task estimates that allow adding time estimates to tasks and running reports that compare estimated versus actual hours to identify overruns and inform future planning. Live capacity tracking shows team utilization in real time during scheduling, with automatic adjustments for part-time staff and public holidays. Task views include Kanban, Gantt, and calendar formats for tracking task progress and visualizing project timelines. Tentative allocations allow assigning resources to potential projects with placeholder bookings that can be adjusted as project scope is confirmed. Integrations include Stripe and Zoom. Covers the agency workflow end-to-end, but the integration list is limited to just two services.

Software 3 is a project management platform for teams and organizations that manage complex projects, track resources, and automate workflows. Pricing starts from $10/user/month (billed annually). A 14-day free trial and free demo are available. It offers project accounting to track budget spend, revenue forecasting, and profit margins across projects and portfolios. Resource capacity planning provides views of team availability and assigns work based on skills, with alerts when someone is overloaded. The portfolio management dashboard monitors progress, budgets, and risks across multiple projects in a single customizable view. A client portal shares project updates, files, and discussions with clients through a free, customizable portal. Integrations include Entity 2 Project, Entity 2 Excel, Entity 4 Drive, Outlook, Entity 3, Entity 8, Entity 7 Online, Entity 9, Trello, and Okta. The project accounting features were relevant, but Software 3 is built for complex enterprise environments rather than the day-to-day rhythms of agency project delivery.

Software 9 is a project management platform built for teams in marketing, operations, and agencies who need to manage complex projects, coordinate tasks across departments, and keep everyone aligned with clear project tracking and collaboration tools. Pricing starts from $6/user/month. A 14-day free trial and free demo are available. Key features include: Creative proofing: Review and mark up creative assets directly in the platform, with centralized feedback to reduce revision rounds; Centralized request intake: Capture incoming work through a standardized request form so every project starts with the right details in place; Project templates: Build and reuse templates tailored to specific workflows so recurring projects launch consistently without manual setup; Gantt chart view: Visualize task sequences, dependencies, and timelines in a single chart to track project schedules across workstreams. Integrations include Box, Dropbox, Everhour, Freshbooks, Github, Entity 4 Drive, Harvest, HubSpot, Entity 8, and Entity 2 Office 365. Good for creative collaboration and project visibility at an affordable price, but no built-in budgeting or profitability tracking.

Software 10 is a cloud-based project management platform for teams and businesses needing to organize tasks, track progress, and collaborate on complex projects. Pricing starts from $4/user/month (billed annually). A free plan, free demo, and 15-day free trial are available. The most affordable option and deeply tied to the Entity 12 ecosystem, but its strength is general task management rather than agency-specific workflows or financial project management.

And then I found Software 6.

Software 6 is a project management platform built for agencies and service businesses that need to manage projects, budgets, resources, and profitability in one place. Pricing starts from $10/user/month (billed annually). A 14-day free trial is available. Key features include: Multiple project views: Switch between Gantt, Kanban board, timeline, calendar, list, and table views to match how your team prefers to work; Resource scheduling: See team capacity, balance workloads, and manage time off requests directly within the resource planner; Project templates: Duplicate full project setups—including tasks, subtasks, descriptions, and assignees—to set up new client projects from a predefined structure; Automated workflows: Trigger actions like creating invoices or sending Entity 9 messages based on project activity, without manual follow-up. Integrations include Entity 8, HubSpot, Xero, Entity 4 Calendar, Sage, Gmail, Entity 2 Outlook, Entity 9, BugHerd, and Entity 7.

What set Software 6 apart was how it answered the question I started with. I needed projects, budgets, resources, and profitability in one platform — and that is exactly what Software 6 is built for. The six project views gave my team more flexibility than any other tool on this list. Resource scheduling with integrated time-off management meant I could plan capacity without checking a separate calendar. Project templates let us duplicate entire project structures for new clients, eliminating the repetitive setup work that slows down every new engagement. And the automated workflows — triggering invoices or Entity 9 notifications based on project activity — closed the loop between delivery and billing without manual intervention.

At $10/user/month, it costs more than Software 10 or Software 9, but the difference is what comes with that price: an integrated financial layer that connects every logged hour directly to project budgets and profitability reports. For an agency or service business, that connection between delivery and financial performance is not a nice-to-have — it is the central operational challenge. Software 6 is the only platform in this comparison that treats it as a first-class feature.
