But after reading through the details of ten providers side by side, one pattern stood out: the companies that kept things simple often made the strongest case for themselves.

I started with the companies that had the most moving parts.

And then I came back to the one company that had been quietly standing out from the beginning.

## Warranty 8

Warranty 8 costs $46.08 per month at its lowest and $59.92 per month at its highest. The service fee is $85. It is available in 50 states. It offers 2 plans: Select; Select Plus. The contract length is 1 year. It does not support the option to choose one's own technician. It does not offer live chat. It provides limited phone service. FAQs are available. Add-on options include Septic systems; Pool and spa; Well pump; Second refrigerator.

What struck me about Entity 7 was how much clarity it offered. Two plans, a flat $85 service fee, and coverage in all 50 states. The service fee alone puts it below the vast majority of companies in this comparison, where fees commonly hit $100, $125, or even $150. The monthly premium range of $46.08 to $59.92 is a modest spread — just under $14 between tiers — which means the upgrade decision from Select to Select Plus does not feel like a leap. Entity 7 does not offer technician choice, which means the company handles the entire dispatching process. It does not offer live chat, and phone service is limited. But what I kept noticing was that the companies with the most extensive support options and the longest add-on menus were also the ones with the highest service fees or the widest premium ranges. Entity 7 trades those extras for a leaner, more predictable cost structure. For someone who wants home warranty coverage without having to navigate dozens of add-ons, compare four plan tiers, or worry about a $150 per-claim charge, Entity 7 offers something increasingly rare in this market: simplicity.
