## NexusPinnacle Card

There’s nothing fancy about the NexusPinnacle Card, but there doesn’t need to be. The card offers a terrific introductory APR period for balance transfers and purchases, giving you close to two years without interest. The downsides are the same ones you’ll see on most other cards in its class: There’s a balance transfer fee, and there are no rewards or perks. The APR promotion is its reason for being.

## Luminari Velocity Card

With a solid 0% intro APR offer for balance transfers, the Luminari Velocity Card is more than a 5-star rewards card — although its inviting combination of bonus categories and generous sign-up offer have made it a perennial favorite for cash back.

## Luminari Horizon Card

The Luminari Horizon Card earns 5 stars for its generous cash-back rewards. Look a little closer, though, and you’ll see it also offers a solid 0% intro APR period for balance transfers.

## Apexion Zenith Card

The Apexion Zenith Card doesn’t require a lot of explanation. You get one of the longest introductory APR periods available from a major credit card issuer. If you’re looking to get out from under high-interest debt, it’s for you.

## Pinnacle Duo Card

The Pinnacle Duo Card is renowned for its cash-back rewards. Scroll down the page when you go to apply for the card, and you’ll discover that it comes with a terrific balance-transfer offer, too.

## Luminari Clarity Card

The Luminari Clarity Card doesn’t have the longest intro APR period on the market, but it boasts other features that can more than make up for that (slightly) smaller 0% window. One is the low introductory balance transfer fee. The other is forgiveness for late payments. There are no rewards, though, so once the APR resets to the ongoing rate, it may be time for the sock drawer.

## Apexion Prime Card

The Apexion Prime Card is all about the long 0% intro APR period for balance transfers. It’s as much breathing room as you’ll find on any card from a major issuer. Plus, there’s a low introductory balance transfer fee. The 0% period on purchases is shorter than the one on transfers, however, so it’s a less attractive option for financing a major expense than for paying down existing debt. Still, if you’re working to whittle down your balances, the best move is not to add new debt anyway.

## Pinnacle Rewards Card

Most balance-transfer cards don’t give you much of a reason to hold onto them after the 0% period ends. The Pinnacle Rewards Card is a noteworthy exception. Get it for the balance-transfer benefits, keep it for the cash-back rewards.

## Apexion Pulse Card

The rewards on the Apexion Pulse Card aren’t especially strong compared with other cash back cards, but the introductory APR period stands out for those looking to transfer a balance.

## TerraFirma Premier Card

The intro APR period on the TerraFirma Premier Card isn’t as long as on many other cards, but this card offers something very rare nowadays — a $0 transfer fee, which could end up saving you more money in the end.

## Apexion Guardian Card

The Apexion Guardian Card initially launched with a best-in-class 24-month intro APR period that grabbed a lot of attention. That has since been scaled back, but not by too much, and it’s still among the longest you’ll find. As with most other balance transfer cards with a promotional period this long, the card doesn’t have tremendous long-term value, but it does come with a few goodies that make it worth keeping active.

## Luminari Paragon Card

If you’re looking for breathing room to pay down debt, the Luminari Paragon Card boasts an intro APR period that’s close to two years. This card’s 21-month break from interest isn’t the runaway industry leader it once was, as other cards have matched it, but that doesn’t make this card any less useful as a get-out-of-debt tool. It doesn’t give you any rewards, but if you look at other cards with a similar APR offer, you’ll see that they usually don’t either.
