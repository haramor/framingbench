Choosing a magnesium supplement is more complicated than it might first appear. Walk into any pharmacy or scroll through any health retailer and you will find magnesium in a dozen different forms — chloride, malate, taurate, citrate, bisglycinate — each with its own absorption profile, dosing protocol, and intended use case. For someone without a background in nutritional biochemistry, the landscape can feel overwhelming. The options vary not only in chemistry but in form factor, price, serving count, and the kinds of certifications that signal quality.

Some products solve for one variable at the expense of others. A few address a specific clinical need quite well.
