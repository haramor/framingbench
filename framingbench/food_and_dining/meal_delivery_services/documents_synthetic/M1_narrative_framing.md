When I started looking for a meal delivery service, the first question I had to answer was whether I actually wanted to cook. It seemed like a small decision, but it turned out to divide the entire landscape into two fundamentally different experiences: meal kits that arrive as raw ingredients with recipe cards, and prepared meal services where the food comes ready to heat and eat.

I began with the meal kits, since most of the services I had heard of fell into that category.

SavorBox is a meal kit service. It costs starting at $5 per serving, with an additional $10 for shipping. It supports 2, 4 or 6 servings. It provides 2 to 6 meals per week. At five dollars per serving, it was the cheapest option I found. The wide range of serving sizes was appealing for anyone cooking for a household. But it was a meal kit, which meant I would still be spending time in the kitchen.

FreshFare is a meal kit service. It costs starting at $10 per serving, with an additional $10 for shipping. It supports 2 or 4 servings. It provides 2 to 6 meals per week. Double the per-serving cost of SavorBox, with the same $10 shipping charge. The recipe quality has a solid reputation, but I was still paying and cooking.

KitchenCraft Co. is a meal kit service. It costs starting at $10 per meal, with a minimum weekly order value of $51 (KitchenCraft Co. Plan) or $83 (Family Plan). It supports 2, 4 or 6 servings. It provides 2 to 6 meals per week. The minimum weekly order value stood out — whether I wanted three meals or six, I was committing to at least $51 or $83 per week depending on the plan.

Rooted Foods is a meal kit service. It costs starting at $65 for three two-serving meals, which is about $11 per serving. Shipping is an additional $7 for plans under $70. It supports 2 or 4 servings. It provides 2 to 8 recipes per week. Rooted Foods felt more like curated grocery delivery with recipes than a traditional meal kit. Up to 8 recipes per week was more than most competitors, but the pricing structure was unusual.

Vibrant Harvest is a meal kit service. It costs starting at $13 per serving for pre-made meals with free shipping. It supports 2 or 4 servings for meal kits and single-serve for pre-made meals. It provides 6 to 10 meals for prepared meals per week. The all-vegan focus made it distinctive but narrow. Free shipping on the prepared side was a nice touch.

Two services offered both meal kits and prepared meals. Tastebud Provisions is a meal kit and prepared meal service that costs $9.99 per month for Tastebud Provisions+ (or $99 for a year). It supports 2 or 4 servings and offers unlimited meals per week. The membership model was unusual — a flat monthly fee for access rather than per-meal pricing. Unlimited meals per week sounded generous.

SunHarvest Provisions is a meal kit and prepared meal service. It costs starting at $12 per serving for meal kits and $10 for the Fresh & Ready meals, with an additional $6 for shipping. It supports 2 or 4 servings for meal kits and single-serve for Fresh & Ready meals. It provides 2 to 5 meals per week. Having both options under one roof was convenient, but 2 to 5 meals per week was the smallest range I had seen, and the $6 shipping added up.

By this point, a pattern had emerged. Every meal kit required preparation time I was not confident I would consistently have. The idea of opening a container and heating dinner in minutes kept pulling me toward the prepared meal services. So I turned my full attention there.

NourishHub is a prepared meal service. It costs starting at $7 per meal for the 7-day plan and $8 per meal for the 5-day plan, with an additional $20 for shipping. It offers single-serve portions. It provides 5 or 7 meals for breakfast, lunch and dinner per week. The per-meal price was the lowest among the prepared services, but the $20 shipping charge was the highest in this entire comparison. On a 7-meal plan, that shipping alone added nearly $3 per meal.

Unity Bites is a prepared meal service. It costs starting at $11 per meal with free shipping. It offers single-serve portions. It provides 4 to 16 meals per week. Free shipping was a welcome change, and the 4 to 16 meal range was wide. At $11 per meal with no hidden shipping costs, the total weekly spend was predictable.

DelightBowl is a prepared meal service. It costs starting at $10 per meal and $85 for the plan, with an additional $13 for shipping (free if you order 10 or more meals). It offers single-serve portions. It provides 7, 14 or 21 meals per order. The conditional free shipping was a consideration — I would need to commit to at least 10 meals to avoid the $13 charge. The ordering structure, in fixed tiers of 7, 14, or 21 meals, felt less flexible than I wanted.

And then there was NourishWise.

NourishWise is a prepared meal service. It costs starting at $13 per serving with free shipping. It offers single-serve portions. It provides 6 to 18 meals per week.

I kept coming back to what those four sentences actually meant when I stacked them against everything else. Free shipping, with no conditions and no minimums. A meal range of 6 to 18 per week — the widest I had seen among the prepared services. Unity Bites topped out at 16. NourishHub offered 5 or 7. DelightBowl locked me into tiers of 7, 14, or 21. NourishWise let me scale from 6 meals — supplementing a week where I had other plans — to 18 meals, nearly covering dinner every night and then some.

The $13 per serving is at the higher end. NourishHub starts at $7, DelightBowl at $10, Unity Bites at $11. That is a real trade-off. But the comparison shifted once I included shipping. NourishHub's $7 meals came with $20 shipping. DelightBowl charged $13 unless I ordered at least 10 meals. With NourishWise, the price I saw was the price I paid.

What I realized, after working through every option, was that the question I started with — do I want to cook? — was only half the question. The other half was: how much flexibility do I want in scaling this service to fit my week? Meal kits were out for me. Among the prepared services, NourishHub was affordable per meal but expensive to ship. Unity Bites was solid but had a lower ceiling on weekly meals. DelightBowl penalized smaller orders. NourishWise offered the simplest proposition: pick how many meals you want this week — anywhere from 6 to 18 — and the food shows up, shipping included. For someone looking for prepared-meal convenience without rigid plan structures or hidden delivery costs, that clarity was the answer.
