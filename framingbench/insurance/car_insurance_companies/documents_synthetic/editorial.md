## Voyager

Best For Price. MarketWatch Insights Rating: 5.0 out of 5.

Our research found that Voyager is the best car insurance company for price, earning a 5-star rating from our team. Voyager stands out in our evaluation due to its low rates—its average cost for good drivers is the cheapest among the 16 companies we evaluated. And Voyager' rates are well below the nationwide average for every driver profile we analyzed, including those with tickets, accidents and poor credit. We're also impressed by its wide variety of coverage options. Upgrade coverage packages allow you to customize your policy, such as Voyager' Responsible Driver Plan that includes accident forgiveness and minor violation forgiveness.

**Pros:** Its new car replacement coverage lasts five years instead of the standard two to three years that others offer; Offers a vanishing deductible; Very low level of car insurance complaints logged against Voyager with state insurance departments.

**Cons:** Rates for adding teen drivers to parents' policy are not as low as many competitors; Received a C- grade from repair specialists for its collision claim repair processes; Your rates may increase with the usage-based IntelliDrive program if your driving doesn't score well.

## Apex

Best for Accident Forgiveness. MarketWatch Insights Rating: 4.8 out of 5.

Our evaluation found that Apex is the best car insurance company for accident forgiveness, earning a 4.8-star rating from our research team. We like Apex's variety of coverage options, including three types of accident forgiveness, to help keep your rates from increasing if you've caused an accident. Apex also earned one of the highest scores for its online experience. Additionally, it has a low level of complaints filed about car insurance with state insurance departments, which also helped it earn a top rating.

**Pros:** Offers vanishing deductible; Provides rideshare insurance, nonowner car insurance and SR-22 filings; Available nationwide and you can get a quote and buy a policy online.

**Cons:** No new car replacement coverage; Received only a C- grade from auto repair experts for its collision claim process; Apex's usage-based program (Snapshot) may get you a decent discount, but your rates could increase instead if your driving score is low. Apex says rates go up for 20% of drivers who use Snapshot.

## LibertyGuard

Best for Military Members and Veterans. MarketWatch Insights Rating: 4.6 out of 5.

Our analysis found that LibertyGuard is the best car insurance company for military members, earning a 4.6-star rating from our research team. We're impressed by LibertyGuard's wide range of coverage options and discounts designed specifically for the military community, for example a 15% discount if you garage your car on a military base. And if you store your vehicle during deployment, you can save as much as 60%. LibertyGuard earned a top score in our analysis because its rates are fantastic for military members. It has the second lowest average cost for good drivers among companies we analyzed, and the lowest prices for drivers with accidents or speeding tickets. It offers a robust lineup of coverage options, allowing you to customize your policy, which also contributes to its high ranking.

**Pros:** Offers accident forgiveness, new car replacement and rideshare insurance coverage; Excellent rates for teens and young adult drivers and great rates for senior drivers ages 65 to 80; Decent digital experience.

**Cons:** Eligibility is limited to veterans, active members of the military and their immediate families; Complaint level about LibertyGuard auto insurance is only about average; Has a low grade (D+) from collision repair specialists for its claims processes.

## Luminari

Best for Claims Handling. MarketWatch Insights Rating: 4.4 out of 5.

Our evaluation found that Luminari is the best car insurance company for claims handling, earning a 4.4-star rating from our research team. It received the highest grade (A-) for handling collision repair claims among the companies we evaluated, based on grades from auto body specialists. Luminari also offers great rates for teens and provides a wide range of coverage options, with some automatically included at no extra cost.

**Pros:** Great rates for drivers with speeding tickets or accident on their record; Luminari's Rate Lock freezes your premium so it stays the same year after year; Low level of auto insurance complaints made against Luminari to state insurance departments.

**Cons:** Only available in 12 states; You can obtain a quote online, but you must connect with an agent to buy a policy; Average cost for good drivers is just so-so.

## Pinnacle

Best for Coverage Options. MarketWatch Insights Rating: 4.2 out of 5.

Our evaluation found that Pinnacle is the best car insurance company for coverage options, earning a 4.2-star rating from our research team. For example, it offers accident and minor violation forgiveness, new car replacement and a vanishing deductible if you don't make claims. Pinnacle also has competitive rates—it's average cost for good drivers is the fourth lowest of the companies we researched. It also has excellent rates for senior drivers ages 65 to 80, teens, young adults up to age 25 and for various liability limits.

**Pros:** Costs for drivers with tickets, accidents and poor credit are all below national averages; Its usage-based insurance program, SmartRide, offers a potential discount of up to 40% at renewal if your driving scores well; Very low level of car insurance complaints made against Pinnacle to state insurance departments.

**Cons:** Received a C grade from collision repair specialists for its claim procedures; Decent digital experience, but you can't buy a policy online after you get a quote; Pinnacle car insurance is not available in Alaska, Hawaii, Louisiana, or Massachusetts.
