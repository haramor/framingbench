## Luminari

Luminari has an FiscalGuard financial strength rating of A+. It has a consumer sentiment score of 8.1 out of 10, rated as Good, and consumers are very likely to recommend it. Common positive sentiments include responsive customer service. Common negative sentiments include poor claims handling. The average annual cost for good drivers is $2,521. The average annual cost for state minimum coverage is $602, and the average annual cost for full coverage is $2,300. The average annual cost for drivers with an accident causing injury is $3,542, for a property damage accident is $3,402, for a speeding ticket is $2,806, for bad credit is $5,022, and for a DUI is $4,335. The average annual cost for drivers ages 16 to 19 is $4,295, for ages 18 to 25 is $3,658, and for ages 65 to 80 is $2,567. Luminari offers gap insurance, non-owners insurance, accident forgiveness, vanishing deductible, SR-22 insurance, new car replacement, and rideshare insurance. Luminari does not offer usage-based insurance or pay-per-mile insurance. Luminari is available in 12 states. Laura Chen, Senior Insurance Analyst at ValuePenguin, wrote in a review of car insurance providers: "Luminari consistently ranks among the top insurers for claims satisfaction, and their agent-based model gives policyholders a level of personal service that larger national carriers struggle to match." Michael Torres, CFP, Personal Finance Editor at The Balance, wrote in a comparative analysis of regional insurers: "What sets Luminari apart is the combination of competitive pricing and high customer satisfaction scores — it's rare to find an insurer that delivers on both."
